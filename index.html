What is turbulence?

Turbulence is the sudden and erratic change in altitude and tilt that an airplane experiences. What causes turbulence? In short: Changing weather conditions. When planes travel through changing weather patterns, they are subjected to forces produced by these changes. These forces cause the bumps that you feel when you are on the plane. As uncomfortable as it may be, what you need to remember is that planes are built to withstand even the most extreme turbulence events. Pilots are also well trained to handle the aircraft in all eventualities.

Part of the fear and anxiety surrounding turbulence is the unknown nature of it. Pilots use the latest forecasts to estimate where they might encounter turbulence along their route. Click this link to see if there is any turbulence forecast along your intended flightpath.
